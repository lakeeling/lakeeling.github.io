Project 1

Design and Development of Learning Systems (student) I am able to design and develop innovative, aesthetic, effective and sustainable learning technologies based on theory-grounded research.
Description: Introduction to Digital Media Final Project Students often need help finding a book to read when they come to the media center. Helping students find books is one of my favorite things to do. There are times when I am busy and can’t help them right away. There are also times when students don’t want to ask for help. Finding a book can be hard for students who don’t know what they are looking for. In the Introduction to Digital Media class, I created an activity students can use to help them find books. Students can use the activity to help them look for books in new ways and in new places. The activity is called “Three Easy Ways to Find a Book.” Students can choose to watch a video to learn how to search for a book in the catalog. They can also choose to listen to me explain why they should ask a teacher, librarian, or friend for help finding a book. I can put a link to this activity on my website or on my social media. The learning activity I designed helps students become more independent in the media center.
Reflection: In this class, we covered a large amount of digital media content including Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, iMovie, and Audacity and the why and how behind it all. I used all of these tools in my activity. I will be able to use a version of the project in my library media center. I would like to make this project more interactive so students can click on parts of the image for information.

Project 2

Front-End Analysis and System Evaluation (student) I developed critical and analytical skills for data collection and evaluation and can reflect and apply these skills to assess and evaluate learning systems.
Description: Instructional Systems Design Project The pandemic has shifted how K-12 schools meet students’ learning needs. The shutdown of 2020 forced teachers to quickly shift to online instruction with little to no training in how to teach online. Since then, Missouri schools are now allowed a certain number of Alternative Methods of Instruction (AMI) days for virtual instruction when they need to close for illness or weather. In the Instructional Systems Design course, my partner and I designed a project to help educators use Canva for instructional materials in combination with their learning management system Canvas. The goal was to streamline how materials are created and presented to students. Student data would be used to determine whether or not students benefited from this streamlined approach. The project allowed us to think through one of the possible ways we could help elementary school students, parents, and education stakeholders navigate the new world of virtual learning.
Reflection: After completing this project, I now have a general understanding of the instructional design process and a good foundation on which to build my knowledge on the subject. This project challenged me to work through the ADDIE Model and think about all steps of instructional design. My project, Canva in the Classroom, was the most extensive assignment I have completed so far in my EdS program. Luckily, I had an excellent project partner, and we worked well together to produce a quality project that I am proud of.

Project 3

Leadership & Social Responsibility (student) I developed competencies for socially responsible technology leadership.
Description: Technology to Enhance Learning Presentation In the Technology to Enhance Learning course, we gave a VoiceThread presentation to our classmates on a technology tool of our choosing. I chose to look for a new-to-me tool that I thought I and/or my coworkers might actually use in the future. I discovered Book Creator, a technology tool that allows teachers and students to create their own digital books, journals, reports, or portfolios. Students can easily collaborate with their classmates or with other students around the world. I enjoyed sharing this technology tool with my classmates and look forward to the possibility of sharing my presentation with teachers at my school.
Reflection: This assignment taught me how important it is to evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of technology tools before using them with students or sharing with teachers. It is more important to be able to learn about new technology tools than it is to try to become an expert in all of them. I will evaluate the resources I purchase for the media center differently and guide other teachers in their choice of technology tools. It will also help me ensure that we make ethical decisions about the types of tools we use and consider student privacy concerns.
